Research Assistant


The Video Wall is a novel teleconferencing system developed at Rutgers University. It is currently used for distance education (for classes conducted between the Newark and New Brunswick campuses). The biggest advantage the VideoWall has over traditional video conferencing systems is that it provides a more realistic platform for a much enhanced and interactive experience, without high time lags.

Before, if a particular member in the audience wants to be a part of the Video Wall conference, they need to be physically present opposite the cameras capturing the streams from one location to the location of the opposite audience. Absentees miss out on the entire meeting. The research project began with the task to display the video wall on a browser.

With a team of four, we worked on displaying the feed on a browser by creating G-Streamer pipelines and WebRTC.
